## What it is

Layout and theming touches for the featured case study card:

- `.featured-case-study_layout` is a two-column grid (content | results) that collapses to one
  column under 768px.
- `.featured-case-study_results-wrapper` is a single-column grid with a `0.25rem` gap for the
  stat rows.
- The dark variant accent: when the card's Webflow component property sets
  `data-wf--featured-case-study-card--style-card-theme="black-olive"`, `<em>` text inside
  `.featured-case-study_title` turns the theme yellow at full opacity, so italicized words in
  the headline read as highlights on the dark background.

## Notes & gotchas

- The `data-wf--…` attribute is **generated by Webflow from the component's variant property**,
  so don't hand-write it; switch the card's theme property in the Designer and the attribute
  (and this accent) follows.
- A commented-out earlier approach (styling `em` via `.is-black-olive` on the component) is
  kept in the file for reference; the variant-attribute selector is the live one.
- The highlight convention: italics in the case study headline are a *color* signal, not
  typographic emphasis, so writers should use them sparingly.
